Projects

12 Focused Interventions Across Tamil Nadu

Each project combines local participation, measurable outcomes, and sustainable pathways for livelihoods, education, and dignity.

1. Land Holding Pattern & Horticulture Prospects, Javvadhu Hills

Duration: 11 months | Client: State Planning Commission, Tamil Nadu

AgricultureLivelihoodsTribal DevelopmentM&E

A comprehensive socio-economic and livelihood assessment among the Malayali Scheduled Tribes in the Javvadhu Hills. The study examined prevailing landholding patterns, agricultural practices, and the scope for horticultural development in the hilly terrain. Special emphasis was placed on identifying opportunities to enhance tribal household incomes through crop diversification and value addition in jackfruit, tamarind, and millets. Consultations with farmers, traditional leaders, and institutions captured indigenous knowledge while analyzing market constraints. Market intelligence studies examined demand trends and buyers. Recommendations included value-chain strengthening, aggregation models, processing facilities, and infrastructure support such as cold storage, transport, and irrigation to reduce post-harvest losses.

2. Agricultural Training Center & Livestock Development Program

Duration: 24 months | Implemented by: Trichy Multi-Purpose Social Service Society | Funded by: Manos Unidas, Spain

AgricultureLivelihoodsM&E

This initiative strengthened capacities of small and marginal farmers in crop management, water conservation, and animal husbandry. A baseline study mapped socio-economic conditions, existing practices, livestock ownership, and challenges. The program supported water committees in drought-prone zones and introduced improved farming methods, soil conservation, and integrated livestock systems. Training covered fodder cultivation, disease management, and breeding. Participatory evaluations enabled farmers and stakeholders to review progress and course-correct. Over 24 months, the intervention improved agricultural output, enhanced food security, and built climate resilience.

3. Surabi Magalir Membattu Iyakkam

Duration: 12 months | Commissioned by: SSH, Dindigul | Location: Dindigul district

Women EmpowermentLivelihoodsM&E

A women-centered program in Dindigul focused on collective empowerment, capacity building, and livelihoods. Baseline research assessed socio-economic conditions, SHG functioning, and barriers to credit, markets, and entitlements. Findings informed a structured plan for group strengthening and cluster-level networking. Training covered leadership, financial literacy, and enterprise development, enabling women to identify viable income-generating activities. The project facilitated convergence with government schemes and local finance institutions for sustainability. Women's decision-making power in households improved alongside economic independence.

4. Kolping India Project

Location: Thiruvannamalai district | Partner: Nandri Foundation

Rural DevelopmentLivelihoodsSanitationWomen

A multi-dimensional rural development project combining socio-economic development with sanitation improvements, livelihood generation, and livestock interventions. Baseline assessment mapped community needs and livelihood gaps. Capacity-building workshops strengthened local organizations and ownership. Income-generation activities such as small enterprises and livestock rearing contributed to measurable household income gains. Sanitation awareness and infrastructure support improved health and hygiene outcomes. Evaluation captured quantitative and qualitative change, including stronger community participation and women-led economic activity.

5. NSDC Handicraft & Skill Development Projects

Duration: 6+ years (48-month core partnership) | Partner: NSDC

Skill DevelopmentYouthWomenLivelihoods

A long-running series of skill development programs to improve employability and incomes of youth, artisans, and marginalized groups. Interventions started with skill-gap assessments and local opportunity mapping, then moved into training in tailoring, embroidery, handicrafts, carpentry, organic farming, and renewable energy. Soft skills, entrepreneurship, and digital literacy were integrated with certification, placement, and market linkages. Artisans were connected to exhibitions and buyer-seller meets. A 48-month NSDC partnership for SC/ST youth trained over 1,480 participants, yielding an average 30% increase in monthly income and supporting micro-enterprises in organic farming and solar services.

6. Organic Farming Livelihood Program (ASCI Partnership)

Duration: 36 months | Partner: Agricultural Sector Skill Council of India (ASCI)

AgricultureLivelihoodsEnvironment

A 36-month program promoting sustainable organic agriculture through training, demonstration plots, and field-level technical support. Modules covered composting, pest management, crop rotation, and certification processes. Demonstrations helped farmers adopt organic methods with confidence. The project benefited more than 400 farmers, with reported crop yield increases of up to 50%. Market linkages enabled access to premium organic buyers and improved incomes. Reduced dependence on chemical inputs supported long-term soil health and environmental sustainability.

7. Child Rights Awareness (with CECOWOR & Child Aid ACA Denmark)

Duration: 24 months | Partners: Child Aid ACA Denmark & CECOWOR | Location: Rural Tamil Nadu

Child RightsEducationGovernance

A two-year initiative to strengthen awareness and protection systems for children in rural Tamil Nadu. Through awareness camps, workshops, and Child Rights Clubs, children learned their rights, leadership, and advocacy. The project also trained duty bearers including teachers, local leaders, and officials on preventing child labor, child marriage, and abuse. Child Rights Clubs were established in more than 50 villages, improving community reporting and response systems. The project embedded child protection into community development practice.

8. Women Empowerment through Skill Development

Partner: BIRDS | Reference: 2022-23

Women EmpowermentSkill DevelopmentLivelihoods

A comprehensive women-focused skill-building initiative in partnership with BIRDS, including tailoring, beautician work, mehendi, and agarbatti-packing. Capacity-building through SHGs added leadership and financial literacy for long-term agency. In 2022-23, over 85 women were trained in tailoring, with nearly half securing placements or starting home-based enterprises. SHGs enabled loan and seed-capital mobilization for micro-businesses. Women's household income contribution and confidence in decision-making increased significantly.

9. Agarbatti & Home-based Livelihoods

Since: 1998 (Foundation support from 2012) | Partner: BIRDS

Women EmpowermentLivelihoods

BIRDS has operated an agarbatti-packing unit since 1998, supporting 10-15 women with steady monthly earnings around Rs 4,000. Hope Ever Foundation has supported this model since 2012 to sustain outreach and income continuity. The program especially benefits widowed, elderly, and low-income women by providing an accessible livelihood option. Additional training in tailoring, mehendi, and beautician trades expanded flexible, home-based income pathways and balanced immediate financial security with aspirational growth.

10. Youth Skill Development - SMILE Twin e-Learning Programme (STeP)

Partner: SMILE Foundation via BIRDS | 1,650+ youth trained

Skill DevelopmentYouthEducation

In collaboration with SMILE Foundation through BIRDS, this youth program trained over 1,650 young people in computer applications, spoken English, soft skills, retail management, Tally, and healthcare (GDA). In 2022-23 alone, 216 youth completed healthcare training, with 126 placed in hospitals, BPOs, and retail firms. Placement rates averaged 60-70%, and many graduates moved to monthly incomes between Rs 8,000 and Rs 15,000. The program reduced unemployment in slums and semi-urban areas while breaking dropout cycles.

11. Healthcare & BFSI Skill Training

Since: 2020 | Partner: BIRDS

HealthcareBFSISkill DevelopmentYouth

Since 2020, more than 500 youth have completed GDA training and secured placements in hospitals, clinics, and home-care services. Training includes practical exposure through internships, improving job readiness. The BFSI track mobilized 233 youth, with 64 placed in banks, NBFCs, and insurance firms. Many participants are first-generation earners supporting household expenses, sibling education, and savings. These programs align disadvantaged youth employment pathways with market demand in essential service sectors.

12. Core Employability & Add-on Programs

Partner: BIRDS

Skill DevelopmentYouthEmployability

Recognizing that technical training alone is not enough, Hope Ever Foundation and BIRDS added employability modules including soft skills, career counseling, and employee engagement. Tally and design tracks prepared learners for accounting, office, and creative roles. Four-wheeler driving linked youth to high-demand logistics and transport jobs. Counseling sessions and employer exposure visits reduced workplace anxiety and clarified realistic career pathways. These add-on modules improve adaptability, confidence, and long-term career resilience.
