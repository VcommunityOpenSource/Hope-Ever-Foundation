Monitoring & Evaluation

Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E)

M&E

Design and implementation of robust M&E frameworks, Social Impact Assessments (SIA), baseline and endline studies, and Social Management Plans (SMPs). Our M&E systems ensure that every program is measurable, transparent, and continuously improving - from project inception through to final impact reporting.

Logical Framework Development Results-Based Monitoring Social Impact Assessment Baseline & Endline Studies MIS Dashboards Mobile-Based Data Collection
Logical framework (logframe) development with measurable indicators
Results-based monitoring systems aligned to program outcomes
Social Impact Assessments (SIA) for development projects
Baseline, midline and endline study design and execution
Social Management Plans (SMPs) for risk mitigation
MIS dashboards for real-time program tracking
Mobile-based data collection tools for field transparency
Donor reporting, impact documentation and audit support
02
Livelihoods & Enterprise

Livelihoods & Enterprise Development

L

From organic farming and renewable energy enterprises to artisan development and business support services - our livelihoods programs build economic resilience for marginalized communities through sustainable, eco-friendly practices. We work alongside farmers, artisans, and rural entrepreneurs to unlock income potential and market access.

Organic Farming Renewable Energy Enterprises Artisan Development Business Development Services Market Linkages Financial Literacy
Organic farming transition - training, demo plots and ASCI-certified curriculum
Renewable energy enterprises: solar pumps, solar dryers, biogas units
Artisan development: weaving, pottery, folk art and product diversification
Business Development Services (BDS): financial literacy and bookkeeping
Branding, packaging and market access support for rural producers
Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) and cooperative formation
Linkage to NABARD, TAHDCO and government livelihood schemes
400+ farmers transitioned to organic farming; up to 50% yield increase
03
Skill Development

Skill Development

S

Industry-aligned vocational training delivered in collaboration with NSDC and ASCI, targeting rural youth, women, SC/ST communities, and differently-abled individuals. Our programs go beyond technical training - integrating soft skills, entrepreneurship, digital literacy, and placement support to ensure graduates succeed in the modern economy.

NSDC Partnership ASCI Certification Healthcare (GDA) BFSI Training Tailoring & Handicrafts Renewable Energy IT-Enabled Services Placement Support
Tailoring, embroidery, handicrafts and carpentry (NSDC-certified)
IT-enabled services, retail management and Tally accounting
Healthcare - General Duty Assistant (GDA): 500+ trained since 2020
BFSI (Banking, Financial Services and Insurance): 233 youth mobilized
Renewable energy skills: solar installation and biogas operation
Soft skills, entrepreneurship development and digital literacy modules
Industry linkages, trade fairs, buyer-seller meets and apprenticeships
1,650+ youth trained; 60-70% average placement rate
SC/ST focus: 1,480 trained under NSDC; 30% average income increase
04
Capacity Building

Capacity Building

C

Strengthening the institutions, groups, and individuals that drive grassroots change. We train Self-Help Groups, Child Rights Clubs, youth collectives, Panchayati Raj institutions, frontline staff, and duty bearers - using participatory, experiential methods that embed skills for the long term rather than one-off workshops.

Self-Help Groups (SHGs) Child Rights Clubs Youth Groups Panchayati Raj Institutions Duty Bearers Project Staff
SHG training: bookkeeping, enterprise management and collective bargaining
Child Rights Clubs formation and leadership in 50+ villages
Youth group mobilization for advocacy and community action
Panchayati Raj institution strengthening for local governance
Duty bearer sensitization: teachers, officials, local leaders
Project staff training in M&E, reporting and field methodologies
Experiential learning, simulation exercises and participatory workshops
Digital tools integration for institutional management and transparency

Research & Planning

Research & Evidence-Based Planning

R

Rigorous, participatory research methods that underpin every decision we make and every program we design. From community-level appraisals to state and national policy research, we generate evidence that drives both local action and systemic change.

Participatory Rural Appraisals Focus Group Discussions Household Surveys Statistical Analysis Policy Research Key Informant Interviews
Participatory Rural Appraisals (PRA) for community needs assessment
Focus Group Discussions (FGDs) with targeted community segments
Key Informant Interviews (KIIs) with government and sector experts
Large-scale household surveys with structured questionnaires
Statistical analysis and data visualization for impact evidence
Policy research for state and national advocacy platforms
Market intelligence studies - price trends, demand and value chains
Socio-economic assessments for project design and donor reporting
