Women Empowerment & Livelihoods

Surabi Magalir Membattu Iyakkam

A women-centered initiative focused on strengthening self-help groups, enhancing financial literacy, promoting enterprise development, and empowering women through collective action and livelihood opportunities.

⏳ Duration: 12 Months 📍 Dindigul District 🤝 SSH, Dindigul

Project Overview

Hope Ever Foundation undertook the baseline assessment and project planning for the Surabi Magalir Membattu Iyakkam initiative, a women-centered program implemented in Dindigul district.

The project aimed to empower women through collective action, capacity building, and livelihood enhancement by strengthening self-help groups and promoting sustainable economic opportunities.

The baseline study focused on understanding the socio-economic conditions of women, the operational status of existing SHGs, and the barriers women faced in accessing credit facilities, markets, and social entitlements.

Based on the findings, a structured project plan was developed to strengthen women’s groups and encourage cluster formation for better networking and collective bargaining power.

Capacity-building sessions were conducted on leadership development, financial literacy, enterprise management, and livelihood planning. Women were encouraged to identify viable income-generating activities that could improve household income and long-term economic independence.

The project also facilitated linkages with government welfare schemes, local financial institutions, and community support systems to ensure sustainability and continued growth.

Through this intervention, women’s participation in household decision-making improved, and their confidence and economic independence were significantly strengthened.

Key Objectives

Strengthen self-help groups through leadership and capacity building.
Improve financial literacy and access to credit facilities.
Promote women-led livelihood and enterprise development.
Facilitate linkages with government schemes and local institutions.
Enhance women’s participation in household and community decision-making.
