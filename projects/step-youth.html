Project Overview

In collaboration with SMILE Foundation through BIRDS, Hope Ever Foundation Trust supported youth-focused skill development programs aimed at improving employability and economic opportunities for underprivileged youth.

Over the years, more than 1,650 young people have been trained in a wide range of skills, including basic computer applications, spoken English, soft skills, retail management, Tally, and healthcare training through the General Duty Assistant (GDA) program.

The training programs focused not only on technical knowledge, but also on communication, confidence-building, workplace readiness, and professional behavior, making participants more competitive in urban and semi-urban job markets.

During the 2022–23 program cycle alone, 216 youth completed healthcare training, out of which 126 secured placements in hospitals, BPOs, and retail companies.

Placement rates consistently averaged between 60–70%, with many graduates earning between ₹8,000 and ₹15,000 per month, representing a major improvement over their pre-training income levels.

Beyond employment outcomes, the program addressed larger social challenges including unemployment, school dropout cycles, and lack of career exposure among youth living in slums and semi-urban communities.

By empowering young people with practical skills, confidence, and career pathways, the initiative has transformed beneficiaries into role models within their communities, encouraging other youth to pursue education, training, and meaningful employment opportunities.

The program continues to demonstrate the transformative role of targeted skill development in promoting dignity, economic mobility, and inclusive social development.

Key Objectives

Improve employability and technical skills among youth.
Provide healthcare, IT, retail, and accounting training opportunities.
Build communication, confidence, and workplace readiness.
Reduce unemployment and dropout cycles in marginalized communities.
Facilitate placements and sustainable career opportunities for youth.
