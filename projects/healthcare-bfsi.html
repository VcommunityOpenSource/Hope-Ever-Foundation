Healthcare & Financial Services Skill Development

Healthcare & BFSI Skill Training

Specialized employability programs preparing disadvantaged youth for careers in healthcare, banking, insurance, and financial services through practical, industry-oriented training.

📍 Tamil Nadu 🤝 Partnership with BIRDS 🏥 500+ Healthcare Trainees

Project Overview

Hope Ever Foundation Trust, in partnership with BIRDS, implemented specialized skill development programs in sectors experiencing rapid employment growth, particularly healthcare and Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance (BFSI).

Since 2020, more than 500 youth have successfully completed General Duty Assistant (GDA) training programs, preparing them for careers in hospitals, clinics, home-care services, and allied healthcare institutions.

The healthcare training included practical exposure, clinical internships, and hospital-based learning experiences, ensuring participants were job-ready upon successful completion of the program.

These initiatives addressed the growing demand for trained healthcare workers while simultaneously creating employment opportunities for youth from economically disadvantaged communities.

In parallel, the BFSI training program mobilized 233 youth, equipping them with skills required for careers in banking, insurance, customer service, and financial operations.

Out of these participants, 64 youth secured placements in banks, non-banking financial companies, and insurance firms, creating stable career pathways in the formal financial sector.

Many beneficiaries became first-generation earners in their families, using their income to support household expenses, contribute to siblings’ education, and begin personal savings.

Through these sector-focused interventions, the programs created sustainable pathways out of economic vulnerability, while also helping industries meet the demand for skilled service professionals in critical sectors.

Key Objectives

Train youth for careers in healthcare and financial services.
Provide industry-oriented practical and internship exposure.
Improve employability among disadvantaged communities.
Support first-generation earners through sustainable careers.
Meet industry demand for skilled healthcare and BFSI professionals.
